Spaghetti Bologneise

Description

Spaghetti bologneise, otherwise also known as spaghetti bolognese, and spaghetti bolognaise, is a pasta and meat dish invented sometime in the last century. Many people consider it to be Italian in origin, but it is not actually considered a true Italian dish in Italy. It has become quite popular in English speaking countries, such as Australia, the USA, and Britain.


I grew up eating a vegan version of this, due to the fact that my parents are vegan, but I now eat the actual meat-version, which in my humble opinion is far superior. Many people add vegetables to the bologneise sauce when cooking it, I do not. I also don't add any salt to the dish, as I'm not a fan of the taste of salt and I prefer to let other people decide how much if any salt they wish to add to their own plate of spaghetti bologneise.


Quick Note:


I don't use your typical measurements for making it, going by eye and feel instead. The below is what I consider the lazy-man's version of bologneise, because I have better things to do then spend hours chopping and cooking. This is a dish I could happily eat every day for the rest of my life.

Ingredients

  • A packet of spaghetti
  • 1/2kg any kind of mince but I prefer using wallaby mince
  • 1 large jar of passata sauce
  • 1 tin of diced tomatos
  • 1 brown onion chopped into small pieces
  • 1/2 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp white or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • A dash of oil for pasta and a dash for the pot you'll cook the bologneise in

Steps

  1. Take a large heavy-based pot, put the dash of oil therein, turn heat up on high, and take your mince. Take the mince with two hands and gradually crumble pieces into the pot, stopping to stir the browning mince every so often, until all the mince is in the pot. Brown the mince. Add the onion. Brown the onion and mince together.
  2. Add the herbs and spices, mixing in, and then add the passata sauce, the tin of tomatos, the sugar and garlic. Add 1/2 a cup of water. Mix everything that is in the pot, and then place a lid on top of the pot, and turn down the heat to low. Leave the mixture to simmer for 1 or 2 hours. When the one or two hours is up, remove the lid but don't turn off the heat yet, and move onto cooking the pasta as outlined below.
  3. Take another pot, fill with water, cold or hot doesn't matter, and place on stove on high heat. Once the water is boiling, add a dash of oil, and add spaghetti. As the spaghetti softens slightly, push it all the way into the pot. Cook for about 8-10 minutes, until it is done. You'll know if it's done, if when you take a piece out of the pot and try eating it, it will taste just right to you. Some people like it softer and others like it with a bit more of a bite, or hardness.
  4. Drain the pasta water from the pot. Take some plates or bowls, and put some spaghetti on the plate, then using a cup or a ladel, put some of the tomato-meat sauce on top of the spaghetti. Sprinkling fresh cheese, or some dried parmesan on top of the dish makes this extra special.
